AI-ready interoperability for education & workforce systems

EDUcore is a shared, semantic reference layer that aligns education and workforce standards into a consistent, governed structure — enabling safe, clear data exchange across the PK20W+ ecosystem.

Why EDUcore?

Education and workforce systems depend on data — but existing standards remain fragmented, overlapping, and difficult to align. Integrations are expensive, brittle, and inconsistent across institutions.

Fragmented standards

Many specs, profiles, and data models exist, but they rarely line up cleanly. Organizations reinvent mappings every time they integrate.

Not AI-ready

AI systems need explicit meaning, provenance, and structure — but today’s specs aren’t designed with semantic clarity or machine reasoning in mind.

Barriers for smaller orgs

Under-resourced schools, states, and employers face high costs and risk simply to exchange data with partners.

What we're building

EDUcore defines a shared semantic backbone using RDF, JSON-LD, and aligned ontologies — supported by governed mappings and clear implementation guidance.

Semantic backbone

A linked-data model grounded in CEDS and credential transparency work, covering key entities like Person, Organization, Credential, and Learning Event.

Use-case-driven mappings

Crosswalks derived from real organizational workflows, versioned and auditable rather than improvised.

Implementation reference library

Developer-friendly definitions, mappings, and guidance that make standards easier to use in real systems.

Safe-by-design architecture

Privacy, provenance, auditability, and human oversight baked in from the start.

Near-term deliverables

Stakeholder registry & resource catalog

A connected network of organizations, standards bodies, experts, and tools across PK20W+ and workforce domains.

Use-case taxonomy

Business-driven use cases mapped to precise RDF-based data elements needed to support them.

Demonstration applications

Working examples showing how AI systems leverage governed, linked semantics safely.

Three-year vision

EDUcore evolves into shared public infrastructure — a trusted semantic layer that reduces risk, cost, and fragmentation across education and workforce systems.

  • Recognized interoperability reference layer used across PK20W+ ecosystems.
  • Production-grade RDF/JSON-LD model aligned with major standards communities.
  • Reusable reference implementations for AI-safe data exchange.
  • Lower barriers for states, districts, colleges, and employers.
